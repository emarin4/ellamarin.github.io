About Me

Hello! I am a first year Astronomy graduate student at the University of Washington currently working with Dr. Nora Shipp. I am fascinated by dark matter, and my work seeks to use both observations and simulations to understand its nature at small scales. Currently, I am leveraging stellar streams and machine learning techniques to constrain the properties of dark subhalos.

I am originally from Connecticut and earned my B.A. in Physics from Dartmouth College in 2025. Outside of research, I enjoy hiking, skiing, and spending time outdoors, and I have loved exploring the Pacific Northwest since moving to Seattle.
